  2. Twilight of the Idols, or, How to Philosophize with a Hammer - Friedrich Nietzsche

    3. One of the first books by Nietzsche that I read from beginning to end. It starts off as a series of aphorisms, then talks about what Nietzsche sees as the problem with post-Scoratic (read: Platonic) greek philosophy. This nicely leads into a discussion about how basing our entire being on pure reason is not necessarily a good thing. (I am not fully stating the extent of Nietzsche's argument so one can read it for themself) The main aim of the book is asking the reader to make sure thier idols are not hollow and to philosphically hit out idols with (philosophical) hammers to make sure they are solid.

  3. Cultish; The Language of Fanaticism - Amanda Montell

    4. Talks about the languages cults use to "brainwash" people. Chock-full of personal anecdotes, practical advice and technical studies, this book is best read in two frames of mind - one, the true crime type where you learn about cults and their methods and two, as an instruction manual learning about how cults draw in people so you can stay safe from those same methods.
